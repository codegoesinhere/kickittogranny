AFL Grand Finals vs Victorian Government (1982 to current)

Background bands show which party formed the Victorian Government on each Grand Final date. Charts exclude the 2010 draw (replay handled as a separate GF).

About

These charts and table data show information around AFL grand finals from 1982 to current.

Last Updated: 14 September 2025

This was created to look at scores, games and results of grand finals in line with:

  • The expansion of the then VFL into other states and then a national competition, the AFL.
  • When did teams outside of Victoria start playing and also win the grand final.
  • What Victorian political party was in government when the grand final took place.
  • Who the Premier was at the time of the grand final and who they support (if known).
  • If there was any obvious alignment of:
    • Political party to scores.
    • Political party to religious sect (club) location.
  • Any other information of interest.

This data is updated infrequently.

Chart 1: Goals (Premiers vs Runners-up)

This chart shows the number of goals scored by both teams in the grand final from 1982 to current.

In most instances, the Grand Final Premier (P) kicks more goals than the Runner-up. Small exceptions include:

  • 1989 - Both Hawthorn (P) and Geelong kicked 21 each.
  • 2006 - Both West Coast (P) and Sydney kicked 12 each.
  • 2010 (Draw) - This match's final score was a draw between Collingwood and St Kilda.
  • 2018 - Both West Coast (P) and Collingwood kicked 11 each.
  • 2023 - Brisbane kicked 13 goals, one more goal than Collingwood (P).

Chart 7: Premiers and Premierships

This chart shows who the Premier of Victoria was at the time of the grand final, and who they support (if known). This is then presented with the AFL Premiers and Runners-up of the respective years.

Changes to football club supporting during a continuous party in government shows a change of Victorian Premier, not a change of allegiance to a football club.

To note:

  • When a Premier was in office:
    • 1990 - Premier Joan Kirner (Essendon supporter), Essendon was the AFL Runner-up.
    • 2010 (Draw) - Premier John Brumby (Collingwood supporter), Collingwood and St Kilda ended in a draw.
    • 2010 (Replay) - Premier John Brumby (Collingwood supporter), Collingwood was the AFL Premier.
    • 2011 - Premier Ted Baillieu (Geelong supporter), Geelong was the AFL Premier.
  • Once a Premier left office:
    • 1993 - Essendon won the grand final after Joan Kirner (Essendon supporter) was defeated in the 1992 election.
    • 2007 - Geelong won the grand final after Steve Bracks (Geelong supporter) retired from politics in July 2007.
    • 2011 - Collingwood was the runner-up after Steve Brumby (Collingwood supporter) was defeated in the 2010 election.

This information is based on Census data for the years listed and VFL/AFL memberships for the relevant year, compared by numbers.

  • Victorian based VFL/AFL club memberships are counted towards Victoria.
  • All VFL/AFL club memberships are counted towards Australia.
  • While members of Victorian based clubs may live outside of Victoria, these form only a small number of members.

To note:

  • Religion names are provided from the Census. This may vary between the years taken and changes made by the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS).
    • n.e.i - not elsewhere included.
    • nfd - not further defined.
  • There is no available information breaking religion down by state for Victoria in the 1986 census.
  • This was used to measure against identifiable and recorded publicly available data.
  • Not everyone would consider AFL a religion, many would.
  • Many would be an AFL member and also have a religion, they would not be mutually exclusive.

Chart 11: Grand Finals by Month

This chart shows that in more than 80% of grand finals in this timeframe are played in September.

This information is framed around a traditional grand final in September, usually played on the last Saturday in September.

This is immortalised in the song by Mike Brady, released in 1980: "One Day in September". Of the two grand finals not covered in this data but relative to song release, both were the last Saturday of September in their respective years:

  • 1980 - 27 September 1980.
  • 1981 - 26 September 1981.

Chart 12: One Day in September

This chart shows when the grand final gets played during the year.

There are two major outlier dates for grand finals in this timeframe:

  • 2000 - The season schedule and subsequent finals was early than usual to avoid a clash with the 2000 Summer Olympics, held in Sydney, Australia.
    • This is also the only time in this timeframe that a September grand final was NOT played on the last Saturday of September.
  • 2020 - The season was impacted by COVID-19. Restrictions, player bubbles and a reduced season pushed the season and grand final back.

Items of interest

Since the competition has broadened from its Victorian roots:

  • 1981 - South Melbourne makes the decision to play all 1982 home games in Sydney, New South Wales.
  • 1982 - In June, South Melbourne was dropped from the team name, becoming "the Swans" for the rest of the season.
  • 1983 - The Swans were renamed the Sydney Swans.
  • 1991 - First Grand Final appearance from a Non-Victorian team, West Coast Eagles.
  • 1991 - First Grand Final appearance from a Western Australian team, West Coast Eagles.
  • 1991 - First Grand Final Runner-up from outside of Victoria, West Coast Eagles.
  • 1991 - First Grand Final Runner-up from a western Western Australian team, West Coast Eagles.
  • 1992 - First Grand Final Premier from outside of Victoria, West Coast Eagles.
  • 1992 - First Grand Final Premier from Western Australia, West Coast Eagles.
  • 1996 - Brisbane Bears and Fitzroy Lions merge to become the Brisbane Lions.
  • 1996 - First Grand Final appearance from New South Wales, Sydney Swans.
  • 1996 - First Grand Final Runner-up from New South Wales, Sydney Swans.
  • 1997 - First Grand Final appearance from South Australia, Adelaide Crows.
  • 1997 - First Grand Final Premier from South Australia, Adelaide Crows.
  • 2001 - First Grand Final appearance from Queensland, Brisbane Lions.
  • 2001 - First Grand Final Premier from Queensland, Brisbane Lions.
  • 2004 - First Grand Final without a Victorian team, Port Adelaide Power and Brisbane Lions.
  • 2005 - First Grand Final Premier from New South Wales, Sydney Swans

As of 27 September 2025, the following Non-Victorian teams have not made a grand final appearance:

  • Fremantle Dockers, Western Australia
  • Gold Coast Suns, Queensland
  • Greater Western Sydney Giants, New South Wales
